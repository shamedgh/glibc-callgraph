6 /* In GLIBC 2.10 HPPA switched from Linuxthreads to NPTL, and in order
7 to maintain ABI compatibility with pthread_cond_t, some care had to be
8 taken.
9 
10 The NPTL pthread_cond_t grew in size. When HPPA switched to NPTL, we
11 dropped the use of ldcw, and switched to the kernel helper routine for
12 compare-and-swap. This allowed HPPA to use the 4-word 16-byte aligned
13 lock words, and alignment words to store the additional pthread_cond_t
14 data. Once organized properly the new NPTL pthread_cond_t was 1 word
15 smaller than the Linuxthreads version.
16 
17 However, we were faced with the case that users may have initialized the
18 pthread_cond_t with PTHREAD_COND_INITIALIZER. In this case, the first
19 four words were set to one, and must be cleared before any NPTL code
20 used these words.
21 
22 We didn't want to use LDCW, because it continues to be a source of bugs
23 when applications memset pthread_cond_t to all zeroes by accident. This
24 works on all other architectures where lock words are unlocked at zero.
25 Remember that because of the semantics of LDCW, a locked word is set to
26 zero, and an unlocked word is set to 1.
27 
28 Instead we used atomic_compare_and_exchange_val_acq, but we couldn't use
29 this on any of the pthread_cond_t words, otherwise it might interfere
30 with the current operation of the structure. To solve this problem we
31 used the left over word.
32 
33 If the stucture was initialized by a legacy Linuxthread
34 PTHREAD_COND_INITIALIZER it contained a 1, and this indicates that the
35 structure requires zeroing for NPTL. The first thread to come upon a
36 pthread_cond_t with a 1 in the __initializer field, will
37 compare-and-swap the value, placing a 2 there which will cause all other
38 threads using the same pthread_cond_t to wait for the completion of the
39 initialization. Lastly, we use a store (with memory barrier) to change
40 __initializer from 2 to 0. Note that the store is strongly ordered, but
41 we use the PA 1.1 compatible form which is ",ma" with zero offset.
42 
43 In the future, when the application is recompiled with NPTL
44 PTHREAD_COND_INITIALIZER it will be a quick compare-and-swap, which
45 fails because __initializer is zero, and the structure will be used as
46 is correctly. */
